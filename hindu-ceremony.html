The Ceremony

Baraat & Milni - Welcome

The celebration begins as the bride's family welcomes the groom and his wedding party (baraat) at the entrance. The Milni ceremony follows, where families exchange garlands and gifts, allowing relatives from both sides to meet and bond for the first time.

Pujas

Once the Groom, his parents and the Bride's parents take their respective places at the Bedi, the marriage ceremony starts with the Ganesha Puja (invocation of the God Ganesha), Omkar Puja, Lakshmi Puja (invocation of the Goddess of Wealth), Kalash Puja (invocation of the God of Water of Varun Devta) and the Navgrah Puja

Jaimala - Exchange of Garlands

The bride welcomes her husband in the traditional manner by garlanding him. The groom in turn garlands his bride. This exchange of garlands is known as the Jaimala

Kanyadaan - Giving Away the Bride

Once all the deities have been worshipped, sacred fire is lit to invoke the blessings of Agni Devta or the God of Fire. Fire forms an integral part of all Hindu customs and rituals, as it symbolizes purity and also acts as a witness to the marriage - in other words Agni Sakshi

The Bride's parents offer their daughter's right hand in marriage to the groom amidst the chanting of sacred mantras and he accepts it.

Havan - Sacred Fire Ceremony

The sacred fire ceremony purifies the atmosphere and creates a spiritual ambiance for the bride and groom's union. Fire represents purity and acts as Agni Devta (the fire god), who serves as the divine messenger carrying their prayers for a blessed married life (Grahast Jivan) to the heavens.

Agni Parikrama - Four Sacred Rounds

Together, the Bride & Groom circle the sacred fire four times, each round strengthening their marital bond. For the first three rounds, the bride leads, embodying Goddess Lakshmi. After the fourth round, she becomes the Ardhangani (wife), symbolizing the equal partnership in marriage. Before each round, the bride's brother offers parched rice to the sacred fire.

  1. Dharma - Religous & Moral Duties
  2. Artha - Wealth & Prosperity
  3. Kaam - Love & Companionship
  4. Moksha - Spiritual Liberation

Saptapadi - Seven Sacred Vows

The most significant ritual where the Bride & Groom take seven steps together, each representing a sacred vow for their married life. These steps mark the beginning of their lifelong journey as partners.

  1. To respect and honor each other
  2. To share each other's joy and sorrow
  3. To trust and be loyal to each other
  4. To cultivate appreciation for knowledge, values, sacrifice and service
  5. To reconfirm their vow of purity, love, family duties and spiritual growth
  6. To follow principles of Dharma (righteousness)
  7. To nurture an eternal bond of friendship and love

Sindoor & Mangalsutra

The groom applies sindoor (vermillion powder) to the bride's hair parting and ties the mangalsutra (sacred necklace) around her neck. These are the final seals of their marriage bond. Five to seven happily married women then bless the bride by applying sindoor to her parted hair, passing on their good wishes for a joyful married life.

Aashirvad - Blessings

With the sacred rituals complete, the Bride & Groom receive blessings from all elders and family members. Everyone present showers them with flower petals, symbolizing their heartfelt wishes for the couple's happiness, prosperity, and a blessed married life ahead.

Bidaai - The New Beginning

The ceremony concludes with the bidaai, as the bride and groom leave the bride's parental home together to begin their new life as husband and wife. Surrounded by the love and well-wishes of both families, they embark on their journey as one, carrying forward the blessings and traditions that have united them.
